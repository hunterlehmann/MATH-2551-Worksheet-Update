\section*{\centering Chapter 13.1: The Geometry of Curves in Space}
\textbf{G3: Geometry of Curves.} I can compute the arc length of a curve in two or three dimensions and apply arc length to solve problems. I can compute normal vectors and curvature for curves in two and three dimensions.  I can interpret these objects geometrically and in applications.\\

\textbf{G5: Parameterization.} I can find parametric equations for common curves, such as line segments, graphs of functions of one variable, circles, and ellipses.  I can match given parametric equations to Cartesian equations and graphs. I can parameterize common surfaces, such as planes, quadric surfaces, and functions of two variables.
